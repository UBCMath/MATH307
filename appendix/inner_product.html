Let \(\boldsymbol{x} , \boldsymbol{y} \in \mathbb{R}^n\). Then

\[ | \boldsymbol{x} \cdot \boldsymbol{y} | \leq \| \boldsymbol{x} \| \| \boldsymbol{y} \| \]

This is called the Cauchy-Schwartz inequality.

Let \(\boldsymbol{x} , \boldsymbol{y} \in \mathbb{R}^n\). Then

\[ \| \boldsymbol{x} + \boldsymbol{y} \| \leq \| \boldsymbol{x} \| + \| \boldsymbol{y} \| \]

This is called the triangle inequality.
